# Statement of Work — Organisational Transformation Consulting Engagement

## Project Overview

**Client:** MediterraneTrade S.p.A. (Port Authority Holding)
**Engagement:** Operating Model Redesign & Organisational Transformation
**Consulting Firm:** Meridian Strategy Partners
**Start Date:** 1 October 2026
**Effort Units:** Hours
**Duration Units:** Weeks

## Objective

MediterraneTrade S.p.A. requires a structured management consulting engagement to redesign its operating model following a recent merger of three port authorities. The engagement encompasses organisational diagnostics, strategic redesign, implementation support, and knowledge transfer to embed the new operating model.

## Scope

The engagement is structured in four phases:

### Phase 1 — Diagnostic (Weeks 1–4)

Assess the current state of the three legacy organisations and identify gaps, redundancies, and quick wins.

**Work Package 1.1 — Current State Assessment**
- 1.1.1 Stakeholder Interviews (C-suite, mid-management): conduct 20 structured interviews across the three entities — 40 h (best 32, likely 40, worst 56)
- 1.1.2 Document & Process Review: review org charts, process maps, governance documents — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)
- 1.1.3 Cultural & Change-Readiness Survey: design and administer a 60-question online survey, analyse results — 16 h (best 12, likely 16, worst 24)

**Work Package 1.2 — Gap Analysis**
- 1.2.1 Functional Redundancy Mapping: cross-map roles and functions across three entities — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)
- 1.2.2 Diagnostic Report: consolidate findings into an executive-ready report — 16 h (best 12, likely 16, worst 24)

### Phase 2 — Strategy Design (Weeks 5–10)

Co-design the future operating model with senior leadership.

**Work Package 2.1 — Target Operating Model (TOM)**
- 2.1.1 Design Workshops (3 × full-day sessions): facilitation, pre-work, and synthesis — 40 h (best 32, likely 40, worst 56)
- 2.1.2 Org Structure Design: define new reporting lines, spans of control, and role profiles — 40 h (best 32, likely 40, worst 64)
- 2.1.3 Governance & Decision Framework: RACI matrix, committee charters, escalation paths — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)

**Work Package 2.2 — Roadmap & Business Case**
- 2.2.1 Transformation Roadmap: phased implementation plan, milestones, KPIs — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)
- 2.2.2 Business Case & ROI Model: quantify savings, FTE impact, and strategic value — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)

### Phase 3 — Implementation Support (Weeks 11–18)

Provide hands-on advisory support during the first wave of transformation.

**Work Package 3.1 — Change Management**
- 3.1.1 Change Management Plan: communications plan, training needs analysis, resistance management — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)
- 3.1.2 Leadership Coaching Sessions (6 sessions × 2 h): prepare senior leaders for new roles — 16 h (best 12, likely 16, worst 24)

**Work Package 3.2 — Process Re-engineering**
- 3.2.1 Priority Process Redesign: redesign 3 end-to-end cross-entity processes — 40 h (best 32, likely 40, worst 64)
- 3.2.2 Interim Performance Dashboard: define and prototype management KPI dashboard — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)

### Phase 4 — Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 19–22)

Ensure the client organisation can sustain and evolve the new model independently.

**Work Package 4.1 — Capability Building**
- 4.1.1 Playbook & Toolkit Delivery: author operating model playbook and toolkits — 24 h (best 16, likely 24, worst 40)
- 4.1.2 Train-the-Trainer Workshop: deliver 2-day internal facilitator training — 16 h (best 12, likely 16, worst 24)
- 4.1.3 Final Review & Sign-off Meeting: present final deliverables, capture lessons learned — 8 h (best 6, likely 8, worst 12)
