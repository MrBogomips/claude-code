# Review Checklist — 8-Dimension Scoring Rubric

Detailed criteria for scoring each dimension on a 1-5 scale. Each score level has specific, observable criteria — avoid subjective judgment.

---

## Dimension 1: Completeness

**What it checks:** Are all expected sections present and substantive?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | All sections present (15 for full, 9 for summary). Every section has substantive content (not just placeholders). Annexes include requirements matrix and glossary. |
| 4 | All critical sections present. 1-2 non-critical sections are thin (e.g., glossary is sparse, annexes incomplete). |
| 3 | 1-2 critical sections missing or placeholder-only (e.g., Risk Management exists but has no entries). Non-critical sections mostly present. |
| 2 | 3+ critical sections missing. Document covers scope and deliverables but lacks governance, risk, or legal terms. |
| 1 | Fewer than half the expected sections present. More of an outline than a SOW. |

**Critical sections** (full mode): Scope (5), Multi-Phase Breakdown (6), Economics (10), Other Terms (14)
**Critical sections** (summary mode): Scope (2), Deliverables (3), Milestones & Billing (4), Governance (7)

---

## Dimension 2: Clarity

**What it checks:** Is the language precise, measurable, and unambiguous?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | No instances of vague language. All requirements are measurable. Scope boundaries are crisp. Every deliverable has binary (pass/fail) acceptance criteria. |
| 4 | 1-3 instances of mildly vague language that could be tightened. All critical requirements are measurable. |
| 3 | 4-6 instances of vague language. Some requirements lack measurement methods. Scope has 1-2 ambiguous boundaries. |
| 2 | Frequent vague language ("as appropriate", "user-friendly", "reasonable efforts"). Multiple requirements without measurable criteria. |
| 1 | Pervasively vague. Scope reads more like a wish list than a specification. Acceptance criteria are subjective throughout. |

**Vague language red flags:** "as needed", "reasonable", "appropriate", "user-friendly", "timely", "adequate", "best efforts", "state of the art", "industry standard" (without specifying which standard).

---

## Dimension 3: Consistency

**What it checks:** Do sections reference each other correctly? Do numbers add up?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Perfect cross-referencing: every deliverable in Scope appears in Phase Breakdown, every milestone in Schedule matches Phase Breakdown, economics align with scope, roles in RACI match team composition. |
| 4 | 1-2 minor inconsistencies (e.g., a deliverable name differs slightly between sections). No numerical mismatches. |
| 3 | 3-5 inconsistencies. Some deliverables appear in one section but not another. A milestone date conflicts with the timeline. |
| 2 | Significant inconsistencies. Scope describes deliverables not in the phase breakdown. Payment schedule doesn't match milestones. Team composition doesn't match RACI roles. |
| 1 | Sections appear written independently. Major contradictions between scope, deliverables, economics, and schedule. |

**Consistency checks:**
- Scope deliverables ↔ Phase Breakdown deliverables ↔ Deliverables table
- Phase dates ↔ Schedule milestones ↔ Payment schedule
- RACI roles ↔ Team composition ↔ Role references in sections
- Risk IDs ↔ Risk references in scope/phase sections
- Assumption IDs ↔ Assumption references

---

## Dimension 4: Risk Coverage

**What it checks:** Are project risks identified, assessed, and addressed?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Comprehensive risk register with project-specific risks. All high risks (P×I ≥ 12) have mitigation plans. Assumptions are explicit with impact-if-invalid. Dependencies have owners and deadlines. Management reserve stated. |
| 4 | Good risk register with mostly project-specific risks. Most high risks have mitigations. Assumptions listed but 1-2 lack impact analysis. |
| 3 | Risk register present but generic (only "scope creep", "delays", "resource turnover"). Some mitigations are vague. Assumptions listed without impact analysis. |
| 2 | Minimal risk coverage. A few risks listed without P×I scoring. No mitigation plans. Assumptions scattered or missing. |
| 1 | No risk section, or risk section is a placeholder. No assumptions documented. Dependencies not identified. |

---

## Dimension 5: Commercial Adequacy

**What it checks:** Are the commercial terms fair, clear, and enforceable?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Payment terms tied to milestones with clear triggers. Change process with SLAs per impact category. Penalties proportionate and capped. Warranty scope and duration specified. Termination conditions include notice period and transition obligations. |
| 4 | Payment terms clear. Change process defined but SLAs could be tighter. Penalties exist but cap is missing. Warranty present. |
| 3 | Payment terms present but not tied to milestones. Change process exists but lacks impact categories. Penalties or warranty missing. |
| 2 | Payment terms vague ("monthly invoicing" without specifying amounts). No change process. Limited legal terms. |
| 1 | No commercial terms beyond a total price. No change process, no penalties, no warranty, no termination clause. |

---

## Dimension 6: Collaboration Model

**What it checks:** Are roles, governance, and communication clearly defined?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Complete RACI with exactly one A per activity. Team composition with allocation %. Ceremonies with purpose and cadence. Escalation path with named individuals and response times. Communication channels defined. |
| 4 | RACI present and mostly correct. Team listed. Ceremonies defined. Escalation path exists but response times are missing. |
| 3 | Roles described but no RACI. Team listed without allocations. Ceremonies mentioned but not formalized. No escalation path. |
| 2 | Roles vaguely described. No team composition. No governance structure. |
| 1 | No collaboration model. Reader has no idea who does what or how decisions are made. |

---

## Dimension 7: Best Practice Adherence

**What it checks:** Does the SOW follow established project management patterns?

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Follows PMI/PMBOK structure. Rolling wave planning (Phase 1 detailed, later indicative). 8/80 rule for work packages. Acceptance criteria per deliverable. Exclusions explicit. Version-controlled. |
| 4 | Good structure. Most best practices followed. 1-2 minor deviations (e.g., all phases equally detailed, some deliverables lack acceptance criteria). |
| 3 | Recognizable SOW structure but several deviations. Missing exclusions. Acceptance criteria inconsistent. No rolling wave planning. |
| 2 | Some SOW conventions followed but structure is ad-hoc. No clear methodology applied. |
| 1 | Does not follow any recognized SOW methodology. More of a project description than a formal SOW. |

---

## Dimension 8: Corporate Standards

**What it checks:** Compliance with provided corporate templates and style guides.

| Score | Criteria |
|:-----:|----------|
| 5 | Full compliance: formatting, terminology, required clauses, branding, numbering conventions all match corporate standard. |
| 4 | Minor deviations: 1-2 terminology differences, slight formatting variance. All required clauses present. |
| 3 | Moderate deviations: missing 1-2 required clauses, inconsistent terminology, formatting partially compliant. |
| 2 | Significant non-compliance: multiple missing clauses, different structure from template, inconsistent terminology. |
| 1 | No alignment with corporate standards. Appears unaware of the template. |
| N/A | No corporate standards provided — dimension excluded from overall score. |
